To start, there are lots of types of flowers — between 350,000 and 400,000 of them. For now, let's look at the top three most beautiful flowers of them all.

Cherry Blossoms

Cherry blossoms come from cherry trees, mainly of the genus Prunus. They're most famous in Japan, where they're called Sakura, but they also grow in Korea, China, the US, and parts of Europe.

DID YOU KNOW
  • Cherry blossom trees can take years to mature and bloom fully.
  • Most cherry blossom trees don't produce edible fruit — the cherries you eat come from different cultivated varieties.
  • Washington D.C. has a famous cherry blossom festival every spring, from trees gifted by Japan in 1912 as a symbol of friendship.

Rosemary

Rosemary (Salvia rosmarinus, formerly Rosmarinus officinalis) is actually an herb, not just a flower — it's part of the mint family, native to the Mediterranean region.

DID YOU KNOW
  • It's one of the most popular cooking herbs in the world, especially in Mediterranean dishes.
  • It's also used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
  • Very drought-resistant — it thrives with little water.

Tulip

Tulips belong to the genus Tulipa, part of the lily family. They're native to Central Asia, but became massively popular in the Netherlands, now world-famous for its tulip fields.

DID YOU KNOW
  • The Netherlands produces billions of tulip bulbs every year.
  • Tulips can continue to grow slightly even after being cut and placed in a vase.

To Wrap Up

Cherry blossoms, native to Japan and other parts of Asia, bloom in soft pink and white shades for just one to two weeks each spring, symbolizing beauty and the fleeting nature of life. Rosemary, a Mediterranean herb with small blue or lavender flowers, blooms mainly in spring and represents remembrance and loyalty, alongside its popular use in cooking. Tulips, originally from Central Asia and now famously grown in the Netherlands, bloom in early to mid-spring with vivid colors like red, yellow, and purple, symbolizing perfect love and elegance. All three — cherry blossoms, rosemary, and tulips — are complete, perfect flowers, meaning each one has petals, sepals, stamens, and a pistil.
